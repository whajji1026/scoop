Zaha Hadid

Iraqi-British architect and painter

"Architecture is no longer a man's world. This idea that women can't think three dimensionally is ridiculous."

About Zaha:

First woman to break the glass ceiling of the "Starchitect" universe, dwelling amongst greats such as Frank Lloyd Wright and Le Corbusier, Iraqi-born Zaha Hadid's pioneering vision challenged notions of what could be achieved in building. Coined the "Queen of the Curve," her highly inventive designs liberated architecture from its traditional treatment of concrete and steel and introduced radical new ways to envision spaces in synchronicity with their surroundings.

With a foundation in painting and the utilization of progressive digital technologies, Hadid's creativity was unbound by existing typologies and her innovative approach helped shift the geometry of buildings toward a radical new aesthetic.

Achievements and storyline:

  • 1950 (october 31st): date of birth
  • 1972: she studied at the Architectural Association in London
  • 1977: she graduated with a Diploma Prize
  • 1982-1983: The Peak Blue Slabs
  • 1989-1993: Vitra Fire Station
  • 1997-2003: Lois & Richard Rosenthal Center for Contemporary Art
  • 1998-2010: MAXXI Museum
  • 2003-2010: Guangzhou Opera House
  • 2005: Phaeno Museum in Wolfsbourg
  • 2005-2011: London Aquatics Centre
  • 2007-2012: Heydar Aliyev Cultural Center
  • 2014: Dongdaemun Design Plaza in Seoul Korea
  • 2016 (Mars 31st): date of death
